About Anandwan

Our History

Anandwan ("Forest of Joy") was established in 1949 by Baba Amte, a lawyer turned social worker who dedicated his life to serving those afflicted with leprosy. Starting with just a few acres of barren land and a handful of patients who were rejected by society, Baba Amte created a self-sufficient community where those affected by leprosy could live with dignity.

Today, Anandwan has grown into a thriving community with:

  • A modern hospital with dedicated leprosy treatment facilities
  • Rehabilitation centers for physical therapy and occupational training
  • Schools and vocational training programs
  • Sustainable agriculture and cottage industries
  • Cultural and artistic activities

Our Mission

Anandwan's mission is to provide comprehensive care to people affected by leprosy through:

  • Early detection and medical treatment: Finding and treating leprosy in its early stages to prevent disabilities
  • Physical rehabilitation: Specialized care to minimize and manage disabilities
  • Social and economic rehabilitation: Training programs and employment opportunities to restore dignity and self-reliance
  • Community education: Raising awareness to reduce stigma and promote early detection
  • Research: Contributing to better understanding and management of leprosy

We believe in empowering patients through self-reliance rather than dependency on charity.

Our Approach to Leprosy Treatment

Leprosy (Hansen's disease) is a chronic infectious disease caused by Mycobacterium leprae. It primarily affects the skin, peripheral nerves, and mucous membranes.

Treatment Regimen

We follow the World Health Organization's Multi-Drug Therapy (MDT) protocol:

  • Paucibacillary (PB) leprosy: 6 months of treatment with two drugs (rifampicin and dapsone)
  • Multibacillary (MB) leprosy: 12 months of treatment with three drugs (rifampicin, clofazimine, and dapsone)
Disability Prevention

Our approach includes:

  • Regular nerve function assessment
  • Early intervention for reactions and neuritis
  • Self-care training for patients
  • Protective footwear and adaptive devices
  • Physical therapy and wound care
Holistic Care

Beyond medical treatment, we provide:

  • Psychological support and counseling
  • Vocational training
  • Social integration programs
  • Community-based rehabilitation

Together, we can create a world where leprosy is no longer a cause of disability, discrimination, and suffering.
